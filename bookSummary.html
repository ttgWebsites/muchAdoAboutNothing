Book Summary

  • So basically...
  • Much Ado About Nothing is perhaps one of Shakespeare's most famous comedies. The play tells the story of two contrasting pairs of lovers. Claudio and Hero are very deeply in love almost to the point that they are left speechless by it. On the other hand Benedick and Beatrice, are both secretly in love with one another, however do not choose to show it, and spend much of the play discussing their scorn of love. In a strange turn of events, Benedick and Beatrice are both tricked into confessing their love for the other, while Claudio is tricked into leaving Hero on the altar. The villain Don John arranges the two tricks, and he nearly gets away with it, however a constable, Dogberry, discovers him. The mystery is resolved and the two couples embrace each other in the celebration of marriage.
