Chapter Summaries

  • Act 1 Scene 1
  • The play opens with the Governor of Messina, Leonato, learning that Don Pedro and the Messina army is returning after a successful journey
  • Leanato is informed of a young man's, Claudio, brave exploits in battle
  • The character Beatrice is introduced as she inquires whether or not Benedick of Padua has survived the battle as well
  • The audience learns that Beatrice despises Benedick as she berates him and compares him to a disease
  • Eventually Don Pedro arrives in Messina with Claudio, Benedick, Balthasar, and Don John
  • Don Pedro and his men have been permitted to stay in Messina for a month by the orders of Leanato
  • Leanato's beautiful daughter is present at their arrival
  • Following the introductions of the men with Leanato, Claudio approaches Benedick and asks him if he took notice of Leanato's daughter Hero
  • Claudio adds that he is very interested in her and would like Benedick's opinion of Hero
  • Don Pedro, who had previously exited stage, returns to find Benedick and Claudio conversing
  • Don Pedro inquires to the nature of their conversation and he learns from Benedick in a mocking tone that Claudio is infatuated with Hero
  • Don Pedro believes that Hero and Claudio would make a good match and chastises Benedick for his mocking of romance
  • Benedick retorts with his desire to remain a bachelor the rest of his life and states that he will never fall in love
  • Don Pedro tells Claudio that he will talk with Leanato about arranging a meeting between Claudio and Hero, however Claudio is afraid of this
  • Don Pedro resolves this by informing Claudio that that evening their will be a masked ball in which Hero will be attending and that he (Don Pedro) will attempt to woo Hero in disguise as Claudio
  • Act 1 Scene 2
  • Don John enters the stage looking sad and dejected
  • Conrad soon follows and approaches Don John questioning his melancholy attitude
  • Conrad believes that Don John should be happy as he has just reconciled with Don Pedro
  • A new character is introduced as Borachio enters the conversation
  • Borachio tells the two that earlier that day he was witness to Claudio and Don Pedro discussing a plan for Claudio to marry Hero
  • Don John asks the two men to follow him and help plan a way to stop any opportunity of marriage occurring
  • Act 2 Scene 1
  • Beatrice is now on stage again speaking with Leonato
  • Beatrice is making shrewd remarks about Benedick and acts very un-lady like
  • Leonato chastises her saying that she will never find a husband if she acts that way
  • Hero and Antonio are now on stage as well
  • The discussion of Hero receiving a marriage proposal comes up and Leonato remarks that if this is true she must accept the offer
  • Antonio chimes in that he two believes she should accept the marriage proposal and Beatrice provides Hero with advice on how to accept the proposal
  • That evening at the masked ball Don Pedro enters wearing a mask and pretending to be Claudio
  • Don Pedro approaches Hero and asks her to dance
  • Hero obliges to the dance
  • Meanwhile Benedick, also wearing a mask, approaches Beatrice and asks her to dance
  • Benedick does not reveal himself and during the dance Beatrice begins to complain about Benedick
  • Benedick responds by telling her he will be sure to inform Benedick about what she thinks of him
  • Across the room Don John and Borachio manage to discover who the real Claudio is and they approach him planning to play a trick on him
  • Don John pretends he believes that Claudio is Benedick and Claudio plays along
  • Don John then goes on to trick Claudio into thinking that Don Pedro is dancing with Hero hoping to woo her for himself
  • Claudio is upset and feels betrayed so he approaches Don Pedro and accuses him of stealing Hero, to which Don Pedro denies the accusations
  • Beatrice returns to the conversation and Don Pedro begins to criticize her for being rude to Benedick
  • Beatrice explains that she was only doing so because Benedick had won her heart before but toyed with her and lost her
  • Don Pedro returns to speaking to Claudio and explains that he was in fact successful wooing Hero and Leonato has even consented to the marriage
  • Beatrice expresses disdain at the belief that everyone is finding love but her, and Don Pedro offers to marry her yet she declines
  • Claudio and Leonato make wedding plans for the next week and in the mean time they plan to make Beatrice and Benedick fall in love
  • Act 2 Scene 2
  • Don John is absolutely livid over the impending marriage as he is very jealous
  • Borachio offers to help Don John prevent the marriage
  • He has a plan that he will use Hero's bed chamber to sleep with his lover Margaret, while Don Pedro and Claudio look upon the window thereby making them believe that Hero has another lover
  • Act 2 Scene 3
  • The scene shifts to Benedick telling himself that he will never fall in love
  • Benedick then sees Claudio and Don Pedro approaching and he hides so he can listen to them
  • Claudio and Don Pedro meet up with Leonato and in doing so they learn that Benedick is hiding
  • Aware of Benedick's presence the men begin to speak of Beatrice's deep (but untrue) love of Benedick
  • Leonato plays along, and eventually as the conversation ends Benedick begins to believe that this is perhaps true
  • Don Pedro (alone on stage) speaks of how they must now have Hero and Ursula do the same thing to Beatrice
  • Benedick returns from hiding vowing to himself that he will return the love to Beatrice
  • Shortly after Beatrice appears and asks Benedick to dinner, she is unaware that Benedick believes she is in love with him
  • Act 3 Scene 1
  • Hero and Ursula make plans to trick Beatrice, they both wait till she is hiding in a nearby orchard and then they approach speaking about Benedick's supposed love for her
  • They discuss how Benedick would never tell of his love because he believes Beatrice would ridicule him
  • Ursula adds that she believes Benedick is a great man and would make an excellent match for Beatrice
  • Act 3 Scene 2
  • It is the day before the wedding and Don John is working hard to prevent it from happening
  • Don John approaches Claudio and Don Pedro and tells them that he believes Hero unfaithful
  • He vows to take them to Hero's bed chamber and prove it
  • Claudio does not particularly believe him but vows that if this is true he will shame her at the church before the wedding
  • Don Pedro decides to take up Don John on the accusations and meet him that night
  • Act 3 Scene 3
  • New characters are introduced, Dogberry and Verges
  • They are both night watchmen, however they decide to pass upon their duties other men for the night and tell them to only seek their help if there is a real matter of disturbance
  • That night the watchmen on duty overhear Borachio and Conrad speaking about how Borachio received a large sum of money for sleeping with Margaret in Hero's chamber and referring to Margaret as Hero
  • He explains that it worked in convincing Claudio and Don Pedro that Hero was sleeping around
  • Act 3 Scene 4
  • Hero prepares for her wedding with the Help of Margaret
  • The two discuss Beatrice and how that someday she may fall in love just like Benedick has with her
  • Act 3 Scene 5
  • Meanwhile across town Dogberry is explaining to Leanato that two men were arrested the night before on suspicious charges
  • Dogberry orders his partner Verges to find someone to write down the story and bring it to Leonato
  • Act 4 Scene 1
  • The wedding party gathers at the church
  • It is approaching the vows and the Friar asks Claudio if he intends to marry Hero
  • To everyone's surprise Claudio announces that he is not, he then goes on to accuse her of infidelity
  • Don Pedro adds that he can not defend Hero either as he also witnessed her infidelity the night before
  • Hero is so distraught by the accusations that she faints
  • Beatrice tries to help Hero but Leonato, ashamed as well, announces that she is dead
  • Leonato announces that he wishes he had never had such a terrible daughter
  • Eventually Hero awakens and she claims that such accusations are untrue
  • Leonato announces that if Hero is lying she will be punished, but if Claudio and Don Pedro's accusations are untrue they will be punished
  • The crowd departs leaving Beatrice and Benedick behind, the two announce their love for each other and Beatrice tells Benedick he must do something to confirm it
  • Benedick must kill Claudio
  • Act 4 Scene 2
  • Dogberry and a town clerk are interrogating Borachio and Conrad about the events the night before
  • They tell the men that they know it was overheard the night before when the two men discussed the plot to disrupt the marriage
  • They also add that Hero has died of shame from the event (this has been made up to trick them)
  • Borachio and Conrad are bound and taken to Leonato
  • He also tells them that Hero died from shame (the friars idea to trick them)
  • The men are bound and ordered to be taken to Leonato.
  • Act 5 Scene 1
  • The play shifts back to Leonato
  • Antonio and Leonato are speaking to one another, and Antonio suggests he make Claudio suffer for the pain he has caused the family
  • Leonato challenges Claudio to a dual
  • Don Pedro however stops this from occurring and Leonato and Antonio leave outraged
  • Benedick then arrives to see Don Pedro and Claudio
  • He challenges Claudio and at first Claudio believes it a joke
  • They soon realize that he is serious and Don Pedro and Claudio determine that this is because Benedick has fallen in love with Beatrice
  • Before a dual occurs Dogberry arrives with the two prisoners
  • Leonato soon follows with the town clerk after he has learned of the scam by Don John
  • Leonato is furious at Borachio, yet he still accuses Claudio and Don Pedro as playing a part in Hero's death
  • Claudio pleads his innocence, but vows to marry Leonato's niece to fix the situation
  • Act 5 Scene 2
  • The play has shifted back to Benedick
  • Benedick is working with Margaret to write a sonnet for Beatrice
  • Beatrice enters and they pronounce their love for one another again
  • They are interrupted by Ursula who comes to inform them that Hero was falsely accused
  • Act 5 Scene 3
  • Claudio and Don Pedro gather with some other men to mourn Hero's death at her gravesite
  • Don Pedro and Claudio bid the other men a good evening and then head off to Leonato's house where Claudio will wed Leonato's niece
  • Act 5 Scene 4
  • Before Claudio and Don Pedro arrive Leonato sends all the women away and tells them to return with masks on
  • Leonato has convinced Antonio to pretend that Hero is really his daughter so that Claudio will believe that he is marrying Hero's cousin instead of Hero
  • Benedick takes the opportunity to ask if he can marry Beatrice, and it is decided both couples will be wed at the same time
  • When the wedding is about to proceed Hero throws off her mask causing Claudio to cry out
  • Benedick questions Beatrice about her love once more and she replies that she doesn't in fact love him
  • They realize that they were set up
  • Claudio and Hero resolve the situation by pulling out the sonnets both Beatrice and Benedick had written for each other pronouncing their love
  • Both marriages go on successfully
  • The play ends as a messenger arrives to announce that Don John has been captured after trying to escape from his crime
